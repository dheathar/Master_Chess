Positioning

Every competitor in this space runs Stockfish and produces, at best, a thin narrative layer on top of engine lines. None of them close the loop from diagnosis to a prioritized, evidence-backed training plan.

✕ Chess.com / Aimchess
Engine analysis with limited narrative. Academy tier is a directory listing, not real multi-student diagnostics. Aimchess is stats-only, no coaching narrative, owned by Chess.com — a conflict of interest for independent academies.
✕ Lichess
Engine lines only, free and open-source. No persistent player model, no coaching narrative, no training prescription.
✕ Chessvia / Chessiro
LLM wrapper over a Stockfish engine. No pattern diagnosis across games, no Academy tier, no B2B offering.
✓ Master Chess
The only platform closing the full Diagnose → Explain → Prescribe loop: a persistent, explainable player model over 23 skills, engine-grounded evidence for every claim, and novel AI-native training mechanisms no competitor has attempted.
♞

The Two Learning Loops

Per the SRS, "learning" happens in exactly two places. The LLM is not one of them — it explains and prescribes, but it never learns and never overrides engine truth.

Player Model & Skill-Tracking Engine

A persistent, structured representation of the player across 23 skills (4 categories, 7 levels), updated by Bayesian Knowledge Tracing from engine-verified evidence in every game.

+

Chess Knowledge Layer

Engine and tablebase ground truth, opening/master-game data, and a 65-title book corpus mapped to the skill taxonomy.

AI Reasoning Layer sits on top of both, producing prose and structuring prescriptions. Provider-abstracted, model-tiered by subscription, and always subordinate to engine ground truth — a runtime claim-guard rejects any generated text that contradicts a verified move or evaluation.

Unseen Functionality — the Differentiators

phased M2–M5

Each of these is a genuine mechanism, not a marketing label — designed for from day one so the schema and pipeline don't need rework later.

♞
Shadow Twin M5

A sparring bot that plays like you. Built from your own error distribution — per phase and skill-demand, the probability of {best, good, inaccuracy, mistake, blunder} — sampled from Stockfish's multipv candidates, contextually reweighted by which skills the position currently demands. A "beat your past self" ladder tracks win rate against successive snapshots of your own error model. The same selector, fed an opponent's profile, powers the Dossier imitation bot.

🔍
Evidence Receipts M2

Every skill score is a claim, and every claim has a receipt. The evidence table logs the exact move behind every rule that fires — click any skill number and jump straight to the moves that prove it. Falls out of the pipeline design for free rather than being bolted on.

↺
Counterfactual Time Machine M5

Branch from any critical moment: "what if I'd played the top engine move instead?" The engine replays forward from the branch, both sides played at matched strength, so you see the position six moves later — not just the one move you missed.

◎
Eval-Calibration Trainer M3

Predict the evaluation before it's revealed. The calibration gap — not raw accuracy — is tracked as its own metacognitive skill, distinguishing chronic overconfidence from chronic blindness, because the fix for each is different.

◈
Opponent-Prep Dossier M5

Ingest an opponent's public games through the identical pipeline, tagged "opponent" instead of "self." Produces a tendency report and feeds a Shadow-Twin-style bot that simulates your actual next opponent for pre-tournament sparring.

⚔
Multi-Coach Debate Panel M4

Ambiguous positions spin up 2-3 coach personas arguing different plans, citing engine lines. The student picks a side and states why; the engine arbitrates. Structured disagreement instead of "just tell me the answer."

⏱
Tilt / Fatigue Telemetry M5

Chess.com and Lichess exports already carry %clk per move. Cross-referencing move time against accuracy surfaces tilt patterns and recommends breaks before they happen again — no wearable required, the signal is already in the PGN.

▲
Plateau-Breaker Programs M5

The five named plateaus (Blunder Wall, Strategy Desert, Conversion Ceiling, Prophylaxis Gap, Precision Boundary) are auto-diagnosed from the skill vector's shape. Each triggers a sequenced multi-week program with a stated hypothesis and a checkpoint re-diagnosis.

✦
Own-Mistake Drills M3

Every drill comes from a position you actually reached and misplayed — not a generic puzzle. SM-2-lite spacing brings it back until it's fixed. The simplest feature here, and the most concretely differentiating one.

Game-Analysis Pipeline

the Beta-milestone core loop
pgnIngest.ts
Splits multi-game exports, parses with chess.js, extracts %clk telemetry, detects player color. Each game parsed in isolation — one malformed game never fails the batch.
enginePool.ts
2-3 Stockfish child processes, least-busy dispatch, auto-restart on crash. Reuses the Stockfish binary already installed for the sibling app.
evalCache.ts
DB-backed FEN→eval cache with an in-memory LRU in front. Opening positions repeat heavily across games — a second upload of the same PGN completes near-instantly.
evaluator.ts
Evaluates every position in a game with bounded concurrency matched to the engine pool size.
classifier.ts
Phase-scaled cp-loss thresholds (tighter in the endgame), win%-dampening at extreme evaluations, missed-forced-mate detection, and a checkmate special-case so the mating move itself always classifies as best.
analysisQueue.ts
In-process job queue, progress persisted for restart-resume, live progress streamed to the client over SSE.
